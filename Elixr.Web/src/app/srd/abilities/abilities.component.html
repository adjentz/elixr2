Abilities

Your abilities are a representation of your character's physical capabilities.

The abilities are: Strength, Agility, Focus, and Charm. Each are explained in detail below.

There are four parts to an Ability: Score, Modifier, Misc., and Bonus

Score

Score is how naturally adapt you are at performing actions related to the Ability.

A commoner would have each Ability score be an average of 10-11. Characters tend to have much higher, heroic, Ability scores.

When you first create your character, each score starts at 0. You then have a pool of 48 Points to distribute between the four scores. To increase a score by one, you reduce the pool by one, until the remaining Ability points is zero.

At this point, you cannot increase a score above an 18, though other sources such as Race or magical items may increase an Ability beyond that.

Modifier

When a score is higher than 11, a creature receives a bonus proportional to the score when attempting an action related to that Ability.

When a score is lower than 10, a creature receives a penalty.

This bonus or penality is referred to as the Modifier, or "Mod", for short.

Modifier is determined by the following table.

Score Modifier
0 Incapacitated
{{ score }} {{ modForScore(score) }}

Modifiers can also be determined by applying this formula:

  1. Take the Ability score.
  2. If the score is odd, round down to the nearest even number.
  3. Take the result of the previous step and reduce it by 10.
  4. Take the result of the previous step and divide it by two.
  5. The result of the previous step is the Modifier.

Misc.

Some magical items or Features give a Miscellaneous bonus to an Ability.

Bonus

Bonus is the sum of Modifier and Misc.

Making an Ability check

Occasionally your GM may call for an Ability check to be rolled, such as an Agility check, or a Charm check. This is typically done if a more specific stat isn't available and the action you're attempting to perform relates most to an Ability.

To roll an Ability check, take the result of 1d20 + Ability Bonus. Your GM will tell you if the result is high enough to perform the action you were attempting.

Strength

Strength represents your Ability to move things physically with your muscles. In pounds, you can:

  • Carry up to 15 times your Strength Score
  • Lift objects above your head that are under 20 times your Strength Score
  • Drag/Push up to 30 times your Strength Score

When carrying more than their capacity (15 times their Strength Score), a character's movement speed is halved.

Agility

Agility represents your Ability to move quickly and quietly. With a high score, you'll be able to:

  • Dodge traps
  • Balance on a ledge
  • Pick a pocket
  • Land gracefully from a fall

Focus

Focus represents your mental abilities. With a high score, you can:

  • Effectively retain knowledge
  • Solve complex puzzles
  • Notice small details
  • Defend your mind against spells

Charm

Charm represents how other people/creatures perceive and respond to you. With a high score, you can:

  • Easily befriend others
  • Bluff your way out of predicaments
  • Have a compelling stage presence
  • Cause others to see your point of view
